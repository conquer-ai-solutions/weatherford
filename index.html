The Use Case

End-to-end procurement.
Three AI entry points.

Sarah mapped the entire procurement workflow and identified specific AI opportunity areas. The goal is to pick one, build an MVP, prove value, then expand.

Procurement workflow overview

The end-to-end flow runs: OQ Creation (via JDE or MRP/DRP) → OQ Processing (Buyer RFQ & supplier selection) → OQ to OR Conversion → OR Approval → OR to PO Conversion → PO Distribution & Supplier Acknowledgement → Buyer/Supplier Date Management → Order Execution.

The workflow spans three business segments: Intercompany, Operations, and Manufacturing/Distribution/Operation MTS — each with different levels of TCS shared services coverage.

AI Opportunity 01 — Beginning of Pipeline
Process B/O to OQ
Automating the processing of backorders into order quantities. This sits at the very start of the procurement pipeline and was identified as an "orange box" opportunity in Sarah's workflow diagram. Primarily in the Manufacturing/Distribution segment.
AI Opportunity 02 — Beginning of Pipeline
OQ Processing Automation
Buyer RFQ and supplier selection — currently manual, labour-intensive work. Sarah's team realised that while the procurement bot (Opp 03) was the initial request, the beginning of the pipeline had more impactful opportunities. This emerged from the deeper analysis.
AI Opportunity 03 — End of Pipeline
Procurement BOT / Exception Messages
The original request from the procurement team. Handling exception messages across categories: T (Transfer), E (Expedite), D (Defer), C (Cancel). Sits at the end of the pipeline. This was the starting point that led Sarah to map the full workflow and identify the upstream opportunities.
Pipeline — Not yet ready
Commercial Bids & Tenders
A second use case involving how the commercial team processes bids and tenders. Still being framed internally — not ready for engagement yet. Monitor and be ready when it reaches engagement-ready status. This is the expansion play.
MVP-first: The agreed approach is to take one of the three orange-box opportunities, build a small MVP, prove value, and earn trust. Then expand to the bigger-impact areas. Don't try to boil the ocean — Wahid was explicit about this.
"I wanna reiterate the fact — something Sarah said — is not reimagining the whole process. Let's say if we were to target maybe an MVP for one of those orange boxes, from a timeline perspective, what are we looking like?" — Wahid Chowdhury, Use Case meeting, 5 Mar 2026
"I'd rather just take something small, create an MVP, and just start to develop trust. You know, and then we can go after some of the bigger impact things." — Conquer response, Use Case meeting, 5 Mar 2026
Our Approach

Discovery first.
Earn the right to build.

Today's kick-off should establish the working rhythm and get us into the procurement SMEs' world. Don't pitch — learn.

Today's meeting goals

1. Introductions: Build rapport with Amiee, Todd, and Sudharshana. Understand their roles in the procurement workflow.

2. Scope confirmation: Confirm which AI opportunity area we're starting with and why. Get buy-in from the new stakeholders.

3. Discovery logistics: Agree on how discovery sessions will work — who we'll talk to, what access we need, cadence of check-ins.

4. Quick wins: Identify if there's anything we can show or demonstrate early to build confidence with the new faces.

Delivery model reminder

Phase 1: Build and test in Conquer's environment with dummy/mock data. No dependencies on Weatherford's systems. Fast iteration.

Phase 2: Deploy into Weatherford's cloud environment and connect to live systems (JDE, MRP/DRP, etc.).

This two-phase approach was presented and well-received in the intro meeting. It de-risks the engagement for Weatherford and lets us move fast without waiting for IT access.

front of mind.

Watch out — TCS overlap: TCS is already moving some procurement activities to low-cost locations. We need to understand exactly where TCS is operating and ensure our AI work complements rather than conflicts with their scope. If we tread on TCS territory, we'll create internal friction.
Don't do this — Scope creep: There are multiple AI opportunity areas and a second use case (bids & tenders) in the pipeline. Today is about confirming one starting point and getting into the work. Do not try to expand scope, pitch on the second use case, or suggest work beyond the agreed MVP approach. Trust is built by delivering, not by promising.
Why this is a strategic account: Weatherford is a $5.5B NASDAQ-listed oilfield services company with 18,000 employees across 75 countries. A successful procurement AI MVP here opens the door to the bids & tenders use case, other functions, and a major enterprise reference. Sarah is a strong champion, the commercial model is agreed, and the use case is clearly scoped. This is one of the cleanest paths to a meaningful engagement we've had.
No NDA on file: Worth noting that there's no NDA in place yet. This hasn't blocked progress so far, but as we move into discovery and start accessing workflow details, this may need to be formalised. Don't raise it unprompted today — but be aware it may come up.
